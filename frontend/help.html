Help & FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions.

How does AiHousing generate reports? ▼
Our system analyses property photos using AI computer vision, then merges them with UK datasets (EPC, crime, flood, land registry sold prices). The result is a clean, readable report designed to highlight risks, defects, renovation ideas and negotiation points.
Do you store my photos? ▼
No. Your photos are processed securely in temporary memory and automatically deleted after the report is generated.
Is this a replacement for a RICS survey? ▼
No. AiHousing provides early-stage analysis to help you shortlist properties. For legal, structural or mortgage requirements, a certified RICS survey is still recommended.
Can I get a refund? ▼
If your report fails to generate due to a technical error, we can issue a refund or credit your account with a free report. Refunds do not apply to successfully generated reports, as analysis costs are consumed.
How long does a report take to generate? ▼
Most reports are generated in under 60 seconds, depending on the number of photos and data availability in your area.
How does the Unlimited plan work? ▼
For £49.89/month, you can generate unlimited AiHousing reports. Cancel anytime directly from your account.
Who can I contact for support? ▼

support@aihousing.uk
What types of properties does AiHousing support? ▼
Houses, flats, bungalows, new-builds, Victorian terraces, and commercial units. If the listing has photos, AiHousing can analyse it.
Is my data secure? ▼
Yes — all data is processed using encrypted connections. We never sell your personal information or store your photos permanently.
Can I upload my own viewing photos? ▼
Absolutely. Upload your own photos to get an even more accurate analysis compared to using Rightmove/Zoopla images alone.
Do you analyse interior and exterior issues? ▼
Yes — damp, cracks, mould patterns, ventilation issues, roof wear, exterior walls, layout concerns, and more.
Do you save my reports? ▼
If you have an account, your reports appear in your dashboard for future access. Free users can download reports but they are not stored long-term.
